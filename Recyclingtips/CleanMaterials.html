Clean Materials Guide

Maximize your recycling value through proper cleaning and preparation

Clean materials can fetch significantly higher prices at junkshops. Follow these cleaning guidelines to ensure you get the best value for your recyclables.

Why Clean Materials Matter

Rinse containers and remove food residue. Clean materials are worth more and are easier to process. Food contamination can reduce value by up to 30%.

Clean, well-prepared materials can get you 20-30% more money compared to dirty, mixed loads.

Material Cleaning Guide

CONTAINERS

Bottles and Cans

Rinse all bottles and cans thoroughly to remove liquids and residues. For sticky substances, use warm soapy water. Remove labels when possible as they can contaminate recycling streams.

Clean aluminum cans can be worth 50% more than dirty ones.

FOOD PACKAGING

Food Containers

Scrape out all food residues before recycling. For greasy pizza boxes, tear off and recycle only the clean parts. Oily contamination can ruin entire batches of recycled paper.

Clean food packaging maintains its material quality and value.

METAL ITEMS

Scrap Metal Cleaning

Remove any non-metal attachments. For copper wire, strip off insulation when possible. Clean metal surfaces of dirt and grease. Separate different metal types for maximum value.

Bare, clean copper wire fetches the highest price per pound.

PAPER PRODUCTS

Paper and Cardboard

Keep paper products dry and free from food stains. Remove plastic windows from envelopes and non-paper attachments. Flatten boxes to save space and prevent moisture retention.

Dry, clean cardboard maintains its fiber quality and gets better rates.

ELECTRONICS

E-Waste Preparation

Wipe down surfaces to remove dust and grime. Remove batteries (recycle separately). Keep components intact - don't break apart unless you know how to properly separate materials.

Well-maintained electronics retain more of their component value.

GENERAL TIPS

Cleaning Best Practices

Use minimal water - a quick rinse is usually sufficient. Air dry materials before storing to prevent mold. Organize by material type after cleaning for efficient recycling.

Properly cleaned and sorted materials can double your recycling income.

Cleaning Do's and Don'ts

  • DO rinse containers immediately after use to prevent residues from drying
  • DO remove caps and lids from bottles (recycle separately)
  • DO shake out excess water to prevent mold in storage
  • DON'T use excessive water - conservation is important
  • DON'T mix clean and dirty materials - contamination spreads
  • DON'T bag recyclables - they should be loose in your bins

Remember: Clean materials mean more money in your pocket and better quality recycling!

Investing a little time in proper cleaning can significantly increase your recycling profits.
