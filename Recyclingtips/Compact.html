Compact When Possible

Maximize your recycling profits by optimizing space and reducing volume

Properly compacting your recyclables can significantly reduce transportation costs and often qualifies you for bulk pricing. Follow these space-saving techniques to get the most value from your materials.

Why Compacting Matters

Flattened and compacted materials take up less space, allowing you to transport more in each load. Many junkshops offer better rates for dense, well-prepared materials that are easier to process.

Compact loads can qualify for bulk pricing, often 15-25% higher than uncompacted materials.

Space-Saving Techniques

CARDBOARD & BOXES

Flatten Boxes

Break down all cardboard boxes completely. Remove any packing materials, tape, or staples. Flatten each piece to create uniform stacks that are easy to bundle and transport.

Flattened cardboard takes up 80% less space and often qualifies for premium rates.

CANS & CONTAINERS

Crush Cans

Rinse aluminum and steel cans, then crush them to reduce volume. You can use a can crusher or simply step on them. Keep different metal types separated for maximum value.

Crushed cans can increase your transport capacity by 3-4 times per load.

LARGE ITEMS

Cut Down Bulky Items

For large plastic containers, furniture, or metal items, cut them into smaller, more manageable pieces when possible. This makes them easier to transport and often reveals more recyclable material.

Properly prepared large items can save you multiple trips to the junkshop.

PLASTIC BOTTLES

Bottle Compression

After rinsing, remove caps and crush plastic bottles. For even more space savings, you can cut the tops off and nest bottles inside each other. Keep different plastic types separated.

Compressed plastic bottles can reduce storage space needed by 75%.

METAL SCRAP

Metal Preparation

Bend or cut large metal pieces to create more compact shapes. Remove any non-metal attachments. Bundle similar metals together with wire or twine for easy handling.

Well-prepared scrap metal often qualifies for higher-grade pricing categories.

GENERAL TIPS

Efficient Packing

Pack materials tightly in your transport containers. Use uniform boxes or bins for easier stacking. Place heavier, compact items at the bottom and lighter, bulkier items on top.

Efficient packing can reduce your transportation costs by up to 40%.

Compacting Do's and Don'ts

  • DO flatten all boxes and cardboard before transporting
  • DO crush cans and containers to save space
  • DO cut large items when safe and practical to do so
  • DON'T compact different material types together
  • DON'T over-compact fragile materials that might lose value
  • DON'T mix clean and dirty materials when compacting

Remember: Proper compaction means more materials per load and higher profits!

A little effort in space optimization can significantly increase your recycling income.
